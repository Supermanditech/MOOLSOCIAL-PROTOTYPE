## 1. Correction

Mool Passport, MoolGraph, Mool Voice, event ledgers and digital-twin-ready data are platform enablers. They contribute only indirectly unless packaged inside something a customer deliberately pays for.

This document defines the directly monetizable technology layer.

A paid technology product must have:

```text
identified payer
specific workspace and business activity
measurable recurring pain
software-controlled result
entitlement and usage boundary
transparent price
customer ROI measure
MoolSocial cost driver
positive contribution-margin path
renewal reason
```

If a feature cannot identify a payer and measurable value, it remains a shared platform capability or free acquisition/retention feature.

## 2. Seven Engines, Not 84 Codebases

MoolSocial builds seven configurable technology engines:

Profiles change schemas, policies, wording and entitlements. Shared modules change once for every compatible profile.

## 3. Direct Revenue Models

1. **Workspace subscription** — per workspace/location/month.
2. **Seat or asset subscription** — staff, doctor, vehicle, device or active outlet.
3. **Usage charge** — AI credits, voice minutes, documents, API calls, lots, messages or jobs.
4. **Transaction platform fee** — completed order, booking, trip, subscription or licence.
5. **Savings or recovery share** — capped share of independently verified saving/recovered money.
6. **Hardware/device lease** — device plus connectivity/support, separately disclosed.
7. **Enterprise/API licence** — organization, geography, throughput, SLA and integration.

Do not charge a subscription, usage fee and percentage for the same undifferentiated value. The plan must explain what each charge pays for and show the maximum where variable.

## 7. Entitlement And Billing Logic

Every technology SKU declares:

```text
eligible profile/activity
payer
workspace/location scope
included modules
included seats/assets/usage
usage meter and freshness
overage price and hard budget cap
trial and conversion event
billing frequency
tax and invoice treatment
pause/cancel/export policy
data retention
support SLA
version and migration
```

State machine:

```text
AVAILABLE
-> TRIAL
-> ACTIVATION_PENDING
-> ACTIVE
-> LIMIT_WARNING
-> LIMIT_REACHED
-> GRACE
-> PAUSED
-> CANCELLATION_SCHEDULED
-> CANCELLED
```

Usage cannot silently exceed a saved hard cap. A plan upgrade or additional charge requires the agreed rule and, where needed, fresh approval.

## 8. Revenue And Unit Economics

Per product:

```text
recognized_revenue
= subscription earned for period
+ accepted metered usage
+ eligible completed transaction fees
+ accepted savings/recovery share
+ earned device/API licence
- refunds and service credits
```

```text
contribution
= recognized_revenue
- payment cost
- cloud/database/storage
- AI/voice/message/maps cost
- onboarding and support
- fraud/refund reserve
- hardware depreciation/connectivity
- partner revenue share
```

A technology product does not scale because it produces gross revenue. It scales only after cohort contribution is positive and support/reliability remains acceptable.

### Illustrative subscription revenue

This is arithmetic, not an adoption forecast:

```text
10,000 Solo/Micro/Growth workspaces x blended INR 700  = INR 70 lakh MRR
 1,000 Growth Operations workspaces x INR 3,000       = INR 30 lakh MRR
   100 Network Operations workspaces x INR 15,000     = INR 15 lakh MRR
                                                        ----------------
Illustrative subscription MRR                          = INR 1.15 crore
Illustrative annualized subscription revenue           = INR 13.8 crore
```

Metered usage, transaction, API, device and savings-share revenue would be additional. GST, refunds, discounts and all operating costs must be excluded or deducted correctly before treating this as recognized revenue or contribution.

## 10. Final Revenue Locks

1. Shared platform foundations are not counted as revenue products until packaged into a paid entitlement.
2. Build seven configurable engines, not 84 independent applications.
3. Every paid SKU identifies payer, price metric, cost driver, measurable ROI and cancellation/export.
4. Basic access to work is never pay-to-apply.
5. Healthcare monetization is technology/administration, not patient referral or clinical outcome.
6. AI is metered only where it creates visible value; no unlimited high-cost AI promise.
7. Transaction fees apply only to the product's completed economic event.
8. Savings/recovery shares use authoritative before/after evidence and a cap.
9. Hardware revenue includes connectivity, replacement, support and depreciation.
10. A product that cannot reach positive contribution or reliable renewal is retired, even if users occasionally tap it.
