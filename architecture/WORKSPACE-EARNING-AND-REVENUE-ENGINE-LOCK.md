# Workspace Earning and Revenue Engine Lock

Date: 2026-07-13
Status: required business contract before production tickets

## 1. Core rule

MoolSocial must increase a participant's net economic outcome, not merely display an earning promise.

Every earning card must show:

- who funds the earning;
- work or sale required;
- payment per verified outcome;
- achievable volume or funded capacity;
- deductions and platform fee;
- release time;
- rejection and dispute rule;
- realistic monthly range based on available funded work, never an unconditional guarantee.

## 2. Funding sources

Earnings may be funded only by:

- manufacturer or supplier sales and marketing budgets;
- retailer, restaurant, service-provider or professional campaign budgets;
- customer-paid goods, services, memberships or delivery;
- MoolSocial launch and acquisition budget;
- MoolSocial advertisement and sponsored-discovery revenue;
- attributable commerce margin or commission;
- workspace subscription and business-service revenue allocated to a funded task;
- creator membership and content revenue pools.

New money is never created by showing an unfunded opportunity.

## 3. Creator earning engine

Creators receive a combined earnings wallet with separate sources:

1. **Campaign work:** fixed fee plus verified outcome bonus from a funded business campaign.
2. **Attributed commerce:** share of eligible net margin from products, food, bookings or services purchased through the creator's content.
3. **Memberships:** monthly or annual subscriber revenue less disclosed payment processing, tax and platform fee.
4. **Content performance pool:** share of eligible advertising or subscription revenue using fraud-adjusted watch time, usefulness, retention and safety.
5. **Local business studio:** paid shorts, videos, menus, product explainers and campaign production for verified workspaces.
6. **Verified onboarding:** bounty only after a referred user or workspace completes verification and a qualifying retained action.
7. **Live and launch events:** host fee, conversion share and audience-quality bonus under approved terms.
8. **Content licensing:** explicit paid permission for a workspace to reuse approved creator content.

Creator earnings should be more attractive than single-source platforms by combining direct fees, recurring memberships, commerce attribution and performance revenue. The client must not promise that every creator will earn more than another platform.

## 4. Consumer and freelancer earning engine

### Consumer

- verified referral and activation tasks;
- demand-aggregation participation rewards funded by supplier savings;
- local verification, survey or proof tasks with explicit consent;
- transition into freelancer, creator or provider workspace without losing consumer access.

Savings, cashback and earnings must be shown separately.

### Freelancer / Earn workspace

- retailer, manufacturer, captain, provider and professional onboarding;
- content creation and local-language education;
- product, shop, menu or catalogue digitisation;
- field verification and evidence capture;
- sales activation and retained-customer outcomes;
- delivery, customer success and campaign execution;
- approved bookkeeping, compliance or professional work where qualified.

Payment may combine a base verified-task fee, quality bonus and retention or revenue-success bonus.

## 5. Retailer earning and value engine

Retailer economic gains include:

- consumer sales margin from app, QR, counter and assisted orders;
- lower procurement cost through aggregated wholesale demand;
- delivery fulfilment income where the retailer uses its own approved delivery;
- manufacturer-funded placement, sampling and campaign incentives;
- verified onboarding and local sales assignments;
- slow-stock liquidation and nearby B2B resale;
- repeat-order and loyalty uplift;
- paid creator campaigns with attributable sales rather than vanity reach.

MoolSocial revenue may come from B2B procurement margin or commission, transaction fee, delivery orchestration, paid workspace services, campaigns, Mool Agent and optional SaaS plans.

## 6. Manufacturer and supplier earning engine

Manufacturer and supplier gains include:

- wholesale sales to verified retailers and business buyers;
- direct demand aggregation and reduced unsold stock;
- area, district, state or national campaign conversion;
- exclusive sales or sourcing contracts;
- distributor, retailer and field-team activation;
- logistics and delivery coordination;
- attributable creator and retailer-led demand;
- raw-material sourcing savings.

MoolSocial revenue may come from wholesale commission or margin, sponsored demand campaigns, logistics orchestration, exclusive sales or sourcing service, business subscriptions, Mool Agent, analytics and professional services.

## 7. Captain and transporter earning engine

- passenger rides;
- food, grocery, medicine and B2B delivery;
- scheduled and return trips;
- verified field, map, photo and onboarding work;
- local campaign execution;
- business transport and porter assignments;
- quality, availability and completion bonuses from funded campaigns.

MoolSocial revenue may come from transparent trip or delivery commission, business logistics fee, campaign fee and optional workspace services. Incentives must be based on completed, fraud-checked outcomes.

## 8. Restaurant, tiffin, cloud-kitchen and salon engine

- orders, subscriptions and bookings;
- repeat plans, memberships and prepaid packages;
- delivery or pickup margin;
- creator-attributed demand;
- local campaigns and offers;
- catering, group ordering and corporate plans;
- off-peak capacity campaigns.

MoolSocial revenue may come from order or booking commission, delivery, campaign spend, workspace subscription, Mool Agent and business services.

## 9. Doctor, clinic, hospital and professional-service engine

- consultation, appointment, follow-up or service revenue;
- eligible care or service plans;
- remote or physical professional delivery;
- administration, scheduling and records tools;
- verified professional workspace subscription;
- customer retention and consented reminders.

MoolSocial revenue should rely on transparent software, booking, administration or service fees permitted for that profession. Referral, prescription or outcome incentives that conflict with professional rules are prohibited pending legal review.

The provider workspace may cover CA, accountant, lawyer and other qualified professionals through capability packs rather than new MVP routes.

## 10. MoolSocial revenue stack

1. B2B procurement margin or commission.
2. B2C product, food, booking and service transaction fee.
3. Ride, delivery and transport orchestration fee.
4. Workspace SaaS subscriptions.
5. Mool Agent monthly subscription.
6. Sponsored discovery and advertisement campaigns.
7. Creator campaign, membership and commerce platform fee.
8. Paid business services plus outcome-based success fee.
9. Exclusive sales, sourcing and territory contracts.
10. Advanced analytics and integrations for verified businesses.

No hidden customer surcharge or sale of personal data is part of the revenue model.

## 11. Economics formula

For every funded outcome:

`net_user_earning = gross_funded_amount - disclosed_platform_fee - payment_cost - applicable_tax_or_withholding - reversals`

`moolsocial_contribution = platform_fee + commercial_margin + subscription_allocation - incentives - payment_cost - delivery_cost - support_cost - refunds - fraud_reserve`

An opportunity cannot be published when its funding balance, maximum payable outcomes or release rules are missing.

## 12. Ten-million-user acquisition loops

The launch target requires parallel loops:

- retailer QR, digital bill and customer-invite activation;
- creator content, membership and attributed-commerce activation;
- captain and freelancer verified onboarding tasks;
- manufacturer-funded retailer and consumer campaigns;
- doctor or provider consented client invites;
- restaurant, tiffin, salon and service repeat-customer invites;
- referral loops released only after a meaningful retained action.

Each loop must report verified activation, day-1 and day-7 retention, first transaction, cost per retained user, fraud loss and contribution margin.

## 13. Admin controls

MoolSocial Admin controls:

- eligible workspace and geography;
- funding account and reserved balance;
- payout formula and cap;
- platform fee and tax treatment;
- start, end and live remaining capacity;
- evidence and quality rule;
- fraud and duplicate rule;
- approval, dispute and clawback policy;
- channel attribution window;
- pause and kill switch.

Users always see the applicable terms before accepting work, publishing a campaign or enabling monetisation.
