## 1. Decision

MoolSocial will not present the same generic service menu to every business. It will diagnose the workspace's activity and current bottleneck, then offer a small set of operated **Outcome Products** specific to that business.

An Outcome Product is not:

- a passive listing;
- an unverified lead pack;
- a directory of agencies or workers;
- a promise of guaranteed sales, income, clinical results or customer behaviour;
- a vague subscription whose work and result cannot be audited.

An Outcome Product combines:

1. work MoolSocial can commit to perform;
2. a business outcome the work is intended to produce;
3. one exact qualifying conversion or accepted milestone;
4. authoritative evidence;
5. disclosed price, variable charge and maximum liability;
6. a completion, exception, dispute and recovery path.

The customer therefore buys a managed result plan, not merely access to tools.

## 2. What “Result” Means

MoolSocial must separate three levels of result:

| Result level | Meaning | Commercial promise |
|---|---|---|
| Operated deliverable | Work under MoolSocial's control, such as digitizing a catalogue, configuring a route, publishing approved campaign assets or reconciling records | May be committed with an SLA, rework or service-credit rule |
| Verified conversion | A customer or business event, such as a retained paid order, completed appointment, accepted delivery, renewed subscription or signed purchase order | Variable fee may be charged only when the disclosed evidence and exclusions pass |
| Business target | Revenue, utilization, savings, recovery or expansion goal influenced by market behaviour | A target, never an unconditional guarantee; customer pays only according to the contracted base work and qualified outcomes |

Views, impressions, clicks, enquiries and raw leads are diagnostic signals. They are not billable conversions unless the customer explicitly buys that exact delivered media outcome and its quality standard is defined.

Every product, plan version and activation must define:

```text

fraud, duplicate, cancellation, refund and return exclusions
acceptance, hold, dispute and compensating-entry rules
SLA, rework or service-credit rule

```

The pricing equation is:

```text
Total payable
= disclosed base operating fee
+ approved third-party spend
+ qualified result fees
+ applicable tax
- service credits

Total payable must never exceed the accepted cap.
```

## 4. 360-Degree Value-Chain Coverage

The recommendation engine must inspect ten pain-point zones. It should offer only the zones relevant to the selected business activity:

1. **Source** — inputs, suppliers, MOQ, landed cost and supply continuity.
2. **Prepare** — identity, licence readiness, catalogue/menu/service definition, price and capacity.
3. **Operate** — inventory, production, staff, slots, routes, jobs and exceptions.
4. **Acquire** — eligible local buyers, guests, customers or business accounts.
5. **Convert** — order, booking, contract, subscription, accepted task or other profile-appropriate event.
6. **Fulfil** — production, packing, appointment, visit, trip, delivery, proof and issue resolution.
7. **Collect** — payment, invoice, receivable, payout and reconciliation.
8. **Retain** — reorder, renewal, refill, revisit, maintenance or recurring contract.
9. **Protect** — compliance, consent, safety, quality, fraud, returns, disputes and reputation.
10. **Improve** — margin, utilization, AOV, route density, repeat rate and next-best action.

No workspace needs ten product cards. Its home should rank the three to five products that address its current constraints across these zones.

## 5. Product Selection Must Be Specific To Business Activity

Profile alone is insufficient. Products are resolved from:

```text
workspace profile
+ industry and business activity
+ goods/service subtype
+ B2C, B2B or mixed operating model
+ home, shop, mobile, delivery, facility or route fulfilment
+ geography and serviceability
+ verified licences and permissions
+ current capacity and stock
+ business maturity
+ measured bottleneck
= eligible outcome products and plan configuration
```

Examples:

- A packaged-flour manufacturer receives retailer territory activation, distributor supply and packaging/input products; a personal-care manufacturer receives compliant assortment, salon/retail channel and creator education products.
- A dine-in restaurant receives table and off-peak capacity products; a delivery-led cloud kitchen receives delivery-radius, kitchen-throughput and packaging products.
- A salon receives staff/slot and membership products; a home beauty provider receives service-area, safe-visit and route-density products.
- A bike captain receives demand-hour and recurring-route products; a fleet operator receives utilization contracts, driver operations and receivable products.

Products and terms are server-configured. The client must not hardcode eligibility, price or regulated authority.

## 9. Recommendation And Diagnostic Rules

The recommender must prefer the constraint that prevents conversion:

```text
No authoritative catalogue/menu/service
-> recommend readiness before campaign

Insufficient stock/capacity
-> recommend sourcing or capacity plan before acquisition

Adequate demand but poor fulfilment
-> recommend fulfilment/SLA product

Completed first orders but weak repeat rate
-> recommend retention product

High sales but weak cash collection
-> recommend receivable/reconciliation product

Regulated profile without verified authority
-> recommend readiness workflow; block transaction activation
```

The recommender may explain why a product is ranked, but it cannot autonomously purchase, reserve funds, publish a campaign, change a price or share protected data.

## 10. Evidence, Attribution And Anti-Abuse

Every qualified conversion must pass:

```text
eligible buyer/participant
authoritative product/service and price
serviceable time and geography
capacity available at acceptance
one contract version
one attribution winner
paid or funded state where required
fulfilment/acceptance evidence
return/refund/cancellation window
fraud and duplicate checks
```

Self-purchase, circular purchase, duplicate identity, fabricated proof, collusive participant/customer activity, unauthorized substitution and split events intended to multiply payouts are excluded.

A later cancellation, refund, chargeback, failed delivery or rejected quality claim creates a hold or compensating ledger entry. Historical records are never silently rewritten.

## 11. Profile-Specific Regulatory Gates

These product families require production legal and operational validation; the architecture deliberately uses conservative gates:

- Food manufacturers, sellers, restaurants, kitchens and tiffin providers require the applicable FSSAI registration/licence and operating controls before eligible food activity. FSSAI states that food business operators must be registered or licensed: https://fssai.gov.in/cms/licensing.php
- Medical practitioner products must not include consideration for procuring or referring a patient. The published NMC Code of Medical Ethics addresses rebates and commissions in patient referral; current applicable professional rules must be revalidated before launch: https://www.nmc.org.in/rules-regulations/code-of-medical-ethics-regulations-2002/1000/
- Pharmacy activation remains licensed, prescription/category and state-law gated. MoolSocial must not infer that a prototype workflow authorizes online medicine sale.
- Ride and transport activation remains state implementation and licence gated. The Ministry of Road Transport and Highways published Motor Vehicle Aggregator Guidelines, 2025, including licensing, driver/vehicle, contract, fare and app concepts: https://morth.nic.in/sites/default/files/circulars_document/MV-Aggregators-Guidelines-2025%20-%20English%20and%20Hindi.pdf
- Personal data, profiling, permissions, purpose consent, access and revocation must be implemented against the applicable DPDP Act/Rules rollout and other sector requirements. MeitY publishes the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025 and enforcement material: https://www.meity.gov.in/documents/act-and-policies/digital-personal-data-protection-rules-2025-gDOxUjMtQWa

This document is a product and architecture contract, not legal advice.

## 14. Final Product Locks

1. Offer outcomes specific to profile and verified business activity, not one generic services catalogue.
2. Guarantee only controllable work; treat market performance as a target with evidence-based result pricing.
3. Never bill a raw lead, view, impression or click as a sale.
4. Never pay for YouTube engagement.
5. Never pay or charge healthcare referral, prescription or clinical-outcome commissions.
6. Disclose base work, third-party spend, result fee, evidence, exclusions, tax and maximum before authorization.
7. Confirm stock, capacity, serviceability and funding before acquisition work begins.
8. Use authoritative domain events, not UI analytics, to charge or pay.
9. Preserve one-tap access to evidence, disputes, reversals and gross-to-net.
10. Render products from one versioned engine with profile/activity adapters; do not create 28 divergent backends.
11. Rank the current bottleneck and show only three to five decisive products on the workspace home.
12. A product is complete only when its promised work is accepted and its downstream end intent has a visible result or explicit recoverable exception.
