# MoolSocial Creator Workspace MVP Lock

Date: 12 July 2026

## Core Boundary

Every MoolSocial account may publish eligible text, photos, Shorts and long-form video from Social. A user does not need a Creator workspace merely to post.

The verified Creator workspace unlocks studio operations, content management, audience insights, campaign eligibility, attribution, commercial disclosures, earnings and payouts. Creator verification must never remove personal consumer or Social access.

## Permanent Creator Dock

`Mool | Create | Studio | Earnings | Chat`

- `Mool` opens the universal personal and social launcher.
- `Create` opens creator publishing tools while reusing the shared upload/transcode/moderation pipeline.
- `Studio` opens Creator Home and the operating routes behind it.
- `Earnings` opens authoritative campaign, attribution and payout records.
- `Chat` opens the shared inbox for people, businesses, campaign conversations and support.

## Monetisation Rule

Views, likes and followers are performance signals, not automatic payout liabilities. A creator becomes payable only under disclosed funded-awareness terms or verified value such as:

- demand originated;
- demand educated;
- demand converted;
- order, booking or subscription converted;
- campaign objective completed;
- other funded and measurable campaign outcomes.

Payout rules, attribution window, geography, content format, disclosure, exclusions, evidence, review and cancellation must be visible before campaign acceptance.

## Monetisation Ladder

MoolSocial should compete through more transparent earning routes, not by promising that every creator will earn more than another platform.

1. **Open publishing:** every eligible user can publish without a Creator workspace.
2. **Verified campaign work:** identity, payout readiness, content-category fit and campaign-specific safety permit micro and established creators to accept funded work without one universal follower threshold.
3. **Attributed commerce:** creator payout can use verified demand, order, booking, subscription or approved outcome attribution.
4. **Channel memberships:** eligible creators may offer monthly and yearly member plans, with optional founding-member pricing and clearly described benefits.
5. **Premium creator access:** later-compatible states may support paid live sessions, premium posts/videos, fan support and creator storefront activity where policy and payment rules permit.

Every earning surface shows gross amount, platform/service charge, payment charge, applicable tax/deduction, creator take-home, payout timing, refund/cancellation effect and dispute route before activation.

## Membership Eligibility

Membership activation is configuration-driven and reviewable. The MVP eligibility resolver considers:

- verified identity, age and payout account;
- at least five eligible original public content items;
- at least 100 followers or a smaller verified returning-audience threshold approved by risk policy;
- no active severe safety, fraud or unresolved rights restriction;
- clear member benefits and delivery capability;
- acceptance of membership billing, cancellation, refund, tax and content terms.

These are launch defaults, not permanent hard-coded promises. MoolSocial may lower or raise thresholds by geography, risk, category and payment readiness, but must show the creator the exact current rule and progress.

Memberships support monthly and yearly billing. Members control renewal and cancellation; creators cannot hide cancellation or retain payment for benefits that were not delivered. Annual plans show the effective monthly price and refund/cancellation treatment before payment.

## Audience Migration

Creators may connect or display public profiles, share their MoolSocial channel link and invite followers through consented channels. MoolSocial must not request external-platform passwords, scrape private follower data or claim imported followers that did not deliberately join.

## Content and Rights

- Uploading is asynchronous and resumable.
- Original file, transcoded variants, captions, thumbnail and moderation state are separate records.
- Draft, scheduled, published, limited, removed and appealed are explicit content states.
- The creator confirms ownership or permission for video, image, audio, people, product and brand material.
- Sponsored or incentivized content requires clear disclosure.
- Regulated categories use policy and compliance gates before campaign visibility.
- Important moderation, demonetisation and campaign decisions are appealable.

## Audience and Privacy

Audience insights are aggregated. The workspace must not expose private viewer identities, personal purchase histories or sensitive attributes to creators. Geography, interest and conversion reporting follows minimum aggregation thresholds.

## Mobile and Web Access

Every Creator route is responsive and available through the mobile app and authenticated web Studio using the same workspace, content IDs, campaign IDs, permissions and backend services.

- Mobile prioritizes recording, camera/gallery upload, quick editing, captions, publishing, comments and live alerts.
- Web prioritizes resumable drag-and-drop upload, large long-form files, thumbnail selection, subtitles, rights details, scheduling, bulk content management, analytics, campaign deliverables and downloadable statements.
- A draft started on mobile can continue on web and vice versa.
- Processing, moderation, publishing and payout states remain identical across devices.
- Web is a first-party responsive application, not a separate creator product or duplicated backend.

## Production Contract

Production uses durable media storage, multipart and resumable upload, asynchronous processing, content and rights records, versioned policy decisions, event-based attribution, double-entry campaign and payout ledgers, explicit campaign acceptance, minimum audience privacy thresholds, role-based team access, idempotent publishing commands and auditable appeals. Mobile and web clients consume the same versioned APIs and realtime state events.
