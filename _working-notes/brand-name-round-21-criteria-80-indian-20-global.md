# Brand Name Round 21 Criteria - 80% Indian, 20% International

Date: 2026-06-25

## Why the last round failed

Round 20 had cultural relation, but the names felt too common, literal, regional, or unprofessional for a large national super-app.

Rejected pattern types:

- Common Sanskrit/Hindi words used directly.
- Names that feel like NGO, government scheme, document app, religious/spiritual brand, local service, or small community platform.
- Words that explain the idea but do not become a premium mouth brand.
- Names that sound meaningful but not fundable, scalable, or app-icon ready.

## New Core Brief

The next name must feel:

- 80% Indian in emotion, mouthfeel, and cultural memory.
- 20% international in polish, scale, spelling, and brand architecture.

This means:

- Indian enough that a chaiwala, auto captain, grocer, family, creator, restaurant, and manufacturer can say it naturally.
- International enough that it can sit beside Zepto, Swiggy, Zomato, Blinkit, PhonePe, Rapido, Meesho, Uber, Amazon, and Google without sounding small.

## What "80% Indian" Means

The name should carry Indian familiarity without using common Indian words directly.

Target signals:

- Soft Indian phonetics: aa, ya, va, ra, na, sa, ta, la.
- Easy two-beat or three-beat pronunciation.
- Can be said in Hindi, Gujarati, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali, Malayalam, Punjabi, and English accents.
- Feels human and warm, not robotic.
- Has hidden relation to Indian ideas like ghar, bazaar, kaam, saath, bharosa, raasta, mandal, vyavahar, seva, samaj, parivaar, udyam, artha, but does not use these words literally.

Avoid:

- Direct dictionary Hindi/Sanskrit.
- Religious/caste/community signals.
- Overused words like seva, sahay, samaj, setu, artha, karya, lok, jan, dhara, pravah, sarthi, saathi.
- Overlocal names that sound like a cooperative society or government program.

## What "20% International" Means

The name should have modern brand polish.

Target signals:

- 5 to 7 letters preferred.
- Clean Roman spelling.
- App icon friendly.
- Works in lowercase and uppercase.
- Can be a verb in speech.
- Does not need translation to feel like a brand.
- Can support product extensions later: Pay, Work, Social, Captain, Shops, Creator, Business, Money.

Avoid:

- Tech cliché endings that feel empty: ly, io, ai, ex, nova, nex, xtra, hub, mart, kart, connect, link.
- Names that feel copied from Silicon Valley.
- Names that feel like crypto, fintech-only, SaaS-only, or logistics-only.

## Platform Objective Fit

The name must fit SuperMandi's core objective:

- Direct operating socio-commerce super-app.
- Consumer always first.
- Work/business roles added as automatic layers.
- Social, commerce, chat, money, and work remain one-tap accessible.
- Products/services appear as decision-ready cards, not endless listings.

The name should therefore imply:

- Life + work together.
- Demand to fulfilment.
- Trust/proof.
- Movement.
- Everyday usefulness.
- Social and commerce together.
- One app that quietly operates everything.

## Sound Rules

Preferred structures:

- CV-CV: two clean beats.
- CV-CV-CV: three soft beats.
- Indian vowel warmth with international spelling.
- No hard clusters that rural or older users may struggle with.

Avoid:

- Too many consonants together.
- X, Q, Z overload unless very natural.
- Ambiguous pronunciation.
- Names where English speakers and Indian users say completely different sounds.

## Brand Feeling Test

A name should pass these sentences:

- "Ispe order kar do."
- "Ispe kaam aa gaya."
- "Payment isi pe bhej."
- "Shop wale ne yahin confirm kiya."
- "Captain ko isi app se ride mili."
- "Creator ne yahin se sell kiya."
- "Manufacturer ko yahin buyer mila."
- "Mummy bhi use kar legi."
- "Investor room me bhi premium lagega."

## Trademark Direction

The best chance is not common words.

Target:

- Invented or semi-invented names.
- Inspired by Indian root sounds but not the exact root word.
- Low public usage.
- No obvious IndiaFilings, MCA, Play Store, App Store, or major global conflict.

## Next Round Format

Round 21 should include 30 names across 15 refined patterns:

1. Indian-root disguised names.
2. Ghar/family inspired but not literal.
3. Bazaar/mandi inspired but premium.
4. Work/action inspired but not "kaam".
5. Trust/proof inspired but not "bharosa".
6. Movement/route inspired but not ride-only.
7. Social/community inspired but not "samaj".
8. Money/value inspired but not fintech-only.
9. Decision/confirmation inspired.
10. Directness/simplicity inspired.
11. Everyday life inspired.
12. Creator/business friendly.
13. Manufacturer/supply friendly.
14. Pan-India mouth-friendly.
15. Premium invented masterbrand.

Each pattern gets exactly two names.

Each name must include:

- Pronunciation.
- Hidden Indian relation.
- International polish reason.
- Platform fit.
- Public-risk note.

## Stronger Rule

Do not present names that are obviously common words.

Do not present names that sound like small local schemes.

Do not present names only because they have meaning.

Only present names that can plausibly become a large brand.
