# Brand Name Round 5 - Paradigm Shift

This round rejects the previous directions:

- not Hindi-sound ending names
- not common-word trust names like Sahi/Sahee
- not category-narrow names like Daili
- not marketplace/chowk/mandi/social-only framing
- not descriptive names that tell the business model too directly

The new naming direction:

SuperMandi needs a coined master consumer brand. The name should behave like a new word that the market fills with meaning over time. It should be able to sit across:

- consumer daily needs
- social media
- creator economy
- auto/taxi captain
- grocer/restaurant
- manufacturer/distributor
- work/earning
- payment/money
- local services

It should be usable in phrases like:

- "___ pe daal"
- "___ se kar"
- "___ kar de"
- "___ business"
- "___ captain"
- "___ creator"
- "___ wallet"

## Names To Discard From This Paradigm

Discard:

- Sahi/Sahee: emotionally right, legally weak and too common.
- Daili: first impression okay, but it narrows toward grocery/household/daily needs.
- Tapp/Kliq/Ping type names: strong mass action, but collision-heavy and legally risky.
- Pure local-place names: they pull the brand back toward marketplace/yellow-pages/chowk perception.

## Current Strongest Recommendation

**Rezli**

Why:

SuperMandi's business model is about resolving demand into action. A consumer need, a social post, a grocery request, a restaurant order, a manufacturer lead, a work task, a ride, a payment, a refund or an earning opportunity all become something the app resolves.

`Rezli` does not trap the brand in one category. It can become:

- Rezli for consumers
- Rezli Business
- Rezli Captain
- Rezli Creator
- Rezli Wallet
- Rezli Work
- Rezli Social

It is short, modern, ownable-feeling, and mouth-friendly enough for India.
