# Brand Name Round 28 - Meaning-First Public Perception

Working note only. Not final legal clearance.

## Why Round 27 Failed

The last round had memory-friendly invented sounds, but the user correctly rejected them:

> People will not perceive meaning of what it is.

This means the name cannot be only brandable. It must give the Indian public an immediate signal of the app's purpose.

New naming objective:

> A name that feels like a brand, but also lets people understand: this app helps me get things, do things, connect, pay, work, buy, sell, trust and operate daily life.

## Core Tradeoff

Pure invented name:
- easier to trademark
- weaker public meaning on day one

Pure common word:
- easier to understand
- harder to trademark
- higher conflict risk

Best route:

> Familiar Indian meaning-root + simple brand form.

No abstract syllables. No cheap slang. No over-Hindi ending. No grocery-only, taxi-only, work-only or payment-only meaning.

## Meaning Territories That Fit SuperMandi

### 1. Direct / Source / No Middle Confusion

This fits SuperMandi's real model because the app is direct operating socio-commerce, not listings.

#### Seedha

Meaning perceived:
- direct
- straight
- no confusion
- from source
- trusted action

Usage:
- "Seedha pe dekh le."
- "Seedha se payment bhej."
- "Seedha pe kaam mil gaya."
- "Seedha se source order mila."

Why it fits:
- consumer can understand it
- manufacturer can understand it
- captain can understand it
- service provider can understand it
- social + commerce + work can all sit under "direct"

Risk:
- common word
- legal search will be difficult
- needs strong visual system to become brand, not adjective

#### SeedhaOne

Meaning perceived:
- one direct app
- direct operating layer

Why it fits:
- more ownable than Seedha alone
- still understandable

Risk:
- "One" may feel corporate if not designed carefully

#### SeedhaMandi

Meaning perceived:
- direct market / direct economic network

Why it fits:
- connected to existing SuperMandi company
- clear commerce signal

Risk:
- may pull public back toward marketplace/ecommerce perception

## My Honest Recommendation

If the name must tell people what it is, the strongest route is:

1. **Seedha**
2. **Karo**
3. **Saath**
4. **Setu**
5. **Pakka**

From these, **Seedha** is the most aligned with SuperMandi's locked principles:

- not marketplace
- not listing
- direct operating system
- consumer first
- role layers added automatically
- trust/proof/fulfilment decision cards
- one-tap social, commerce, work, money and chat

However, because these are meaning-first names, legal clearance will be harder than with invented names. We should treat this round as territory selection before IP search.
