# Brand Name Round 19 - Professional Masterbrand Direction

Date: 2026-06-25

## Why this round exists

The previous direction had the correct business context but sounded too much like a local utility slogan:

- Seedho
- KaamHo
- HoGaya
- KaamSet
- SabSet

Those can work as campaign language or microcopy, but not as the main national app brand.

The new direction is:

- Professional masterbrand first.
- Human promise second.
- Short, ownable, app-icon friendly name.
- Not too Hindi, not too English, not religious, not grocery-only, not taxi-only, not fintech-only.
- Should work for consumer, auto captain, grocer, restaurant, creator, manufacturer, worker, money, chat, and social use.

## Legal and screening caveat

This is a creative and preliminary public-risk round, not a legal clearance.

Before locking any name, run:

- Exact IP India trademark search.
- Phonetic IP India trademark search.
- Class screening at least for 9, 35, 36, 38, 39, 41, 42, and likely adjacent classes.
- MCA company/LLP name check.
- Google Play / App Store check.
- Domain and social handle check.
- Trademark attorney review.

## Important public-risk learning from quick screening

Many professional-looking names already have visible noise:

- Mavon has an IndiaFilings record in Class 3 with status removed.
- Navyo has a visible Indian company reference.
- Ruvio has a registered IndiaFilings record in Class 3.
- Nivon has several IndiaFilings records and an old Indian textile/company presence.
- Ayvo has an IndiaFilings record and an Indian company reference.
- Avoro, Zavio, Zevra, Zendo, Velora, Vaya, Vero, Navo, Oriva, Omera, Yonda, Yara all have visible public conflicts or heavy noise.

This confirms that the final name should be screened before emotional attachment.

## Current best direction

The best direction from this round is not a single final name yet. It is a naming shape:

1. Five letters preferred.
2. Two easy spoken beats.
3. Ends in open sound: o / a / yo.
4. Avoid literal Hindi utility words.
5. Avoid too much Sanskrit/religious feel.
6. Avoid names that sound like only grocery, only ride, only money, or only social.
7. Must sound credible next to payments, logistics, verified work, and business tools.

## Names to test aloud first

Say these in real Indian usage:

- "Mavon kar do."
- "Movro pe check karo."
- "Navyo pe order kar."
- "Vunio me payment bhej."
- "Varmo se kaam le lo."

If the name cannot survive chai-stall, auto-stand, shop-counter, and investor-room pronunciation, reject it.

## My current ranked view

1. Movro - strongest professional operating energy, but phonetic risk with Avro/Mavro.
2. Mavon - most balanced, but public record exists.
3. Navyo - easiest mouthfeel, but company reference exists.
4. Varmo - serious and calm, but less emotional.
5. Vunio - broader platform feeling, but pronunciation risk.

None are lock-ready.

## Next recommended move

Run one more round using only this improved shape:

- Five letters.
- Two spoken beats.
- No existing obvious Indian company or app hit.
- Not a dictionary/common word.
- Not a slogan.
- Not a religious/caste/culture-heavy name.
- Strong enough for finance, logistics, work, and social.

The next round should be stricter and should remove any candidate that shows an obvious IndiaFilings, MCA-style, Google Play, or major global mark conflict.
