# Brand Name Round 18 - Professional Reset

Date: 2026-06-25

Purpose:

Correct the naming direction after feedback that the previous names had the right context but felt unprofessional.

## Important Correction

The emotional promise is right:

"Kaam ho gaya."

But the app name should not sound like the promise line itself.

Names like these are now rejected as main app names:

- Seedho
- KaamHo
- HoGaya
- HoJaye
- SahiHo
- PakkaHo
- PooraHo
- KaamSet
- SabSet

Reason:

They are understandable, but they sound like slogans, utility commands, or local service apps. They do not yet feel like a national, fundable, trademarkable, super-app masterbrand.

## New Professional Naming Rule

Use this structure:

**Professional masterbrand + human promise**

Example structure:

- Brand name: polished, short, ownable, app-icon friendly
- Tagline / public promise: "Kaam ho gaya" / "Life, work and money in one direct app"

The brand name should carry the emotion quietly, not literally.

## What The Name Must Feel Like

The name should feel:

- trustworthy
- national
- modern
- direct
- operational
- app-first
- premium enough for money, logistics, work and business tools
- friendly enough for consumer, auto captain, chaiwala, grocer, creator and family users

## What To Avoid Now

Reject names that sound like:

- local errand service
- slogan
- government scheme
- NGO
- generic Hindi command
- grocery app
- delivery-only app
- payment-only app
- taxi-only app
- forced startup syllables with no soul

## New Candidate Style

The next round should use polished coined names with hidden meaning.

Better patterns:

- "done" energy, but not the word done
- "direct" energy, but not the word direct
- "trust" energy, but not literal bharosa
- "together" energy, but not literal saath
- "life/work flow" energy, but not generic flow

## Current Working Direction

Do not continue with the literal Hindi / Hinglish phrase names.

Move toward names that feel like:

- Zepto-level shortness
- Swiggy-level mouth recall
- Rapido-level action
- PhonePe-level trust/action clarity
- Urban Company-level professional scope
- Meesho-level mass friendliness

But avoid copying their sound.

## Next Round Requirement

Each next candidate must include:

- pronunciation
- hidden meaning
- why it feels professional
- why it can work for all user types
- quick public-risk note
- possible tagline
