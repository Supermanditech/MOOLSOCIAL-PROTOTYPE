## Master-Plan Naming Implications

The name must support:

- Consumer-first entry.
- Social, chat, money, utility, commerce and work in one app.
- Direct operating socio-commerce, not marketplace listing.
- Local execution, geo readiness, QR nodes and offline trust.
- Verified work and payout discipline without fake income promise.
- Business, professional and worker layers without removing personal consumer access.
- Future expansion into procurement, campaigns, source-side supply, fulfilment and operating intelligence.

The name should not sound like:

- Only grocery.
- Only wholesale.
- Only social media.
- Only earning/jobs.
- Only AI assistant.
- Only marketplace/yellow pages.

## Candidate Assessment

### SuperMandi

Recommendation: keep as master brand.

Strengths:

- Already aligned with the company identity and existing planning language.
- Familiar India-local word: "mandi" signals market, demand, trade, price and local economic life.
- "Super" expands the meaning beyond a normal mandi into a super-app.
- Works for consumer, business, worker and offline QR acquisition.
- Short, memorable and easy to speak.

Risks:

- "Mandi" may make users think only vegetable/grocery/wholesale market.
- It may sound like a marketplace unless the tagline, first screen and logo clarify direct operation.
- It may not immediately communicate social, chat, money and work.

Mitigation:

- Use a broad public descriptor everywhere:
  `Pay. Buy. Book. Work.`
- Use the first screen name:
  `Today`
- Avoid marketplace visuals such as only cart, basket, shop shelf or product grid.
- Logo should show operating trust/network/value, not only shopping.

### SuperMandi Today

Recommendation: use as product surface/screen language, not app name.

Use:

- Home tab: `Today`
- Screenbook language: `SuperMandi Today`
- Dashboard concept: what can I pay, buy, book, earn, chat or act on now?

Do not use as Play Store name unless later needed, because it is longer.

### Other Name Families Considered

Not recommended now:

- MandiOne: too marketplace-like.
- ApnaMandi: too generic and likely crowded.
- KaamMandi: too work-only.
- OutcomeMandi: too technical.
- LokMandi: community/trade feel, but weaker for bills, money and app utility.
- SuperOutcome: too abstract and not consumer-friendly.

## Recommended Naming Architecture

Company/legal:

`SuperMandi Tech Private Limited`

Consumer app display name:

`SuperMandi`

Home screen:

`Today`

Public tagline:

`Pay. Buy. Book. Work.`

Long app positioning:

`Pay bills, buy daily needs, book local services, chat, use money tools and earn from verified work where available.`

Internal architecture terms:

Never visible to users:

- Demand Vault
- Campaign Engine
- Matching Engine
- Ledger Unit
- Dependency Graph
- Geo Outcome Unit

## Logo Direction

Logo should communicate:

- Trust.
- Local value.
- Movement from need to action.
- Money/payment readiness.
- Verified outcome.
- Super-app breadth.

Avoid:

- Shopping cart-only logo.
- Vegetable basket-only logo.
- Classified/listing-style icon.
- AI robot face.
- Too many tiny symbols.

Recommended visual system:

- Primary mark: strong `SM` monogram.
- Supporting symbol: four connected action nodes representing Pay, Buy, Book, Work.
- Optional trust cue: small check/receipt form inside the mark.
- Color direction: trust green + deep ink/navy + warm amber accent.

## Decision

Working recommendation:

Keep `SuperMandi` as the final master brand unless legal/trademark clearance blocks it.

Use `Today` as the universal first-screen name.

Use `Pay. Buy. Book. Work.` as the public shorthand so the brand does not collapse into grocery/marketplace meaning.
