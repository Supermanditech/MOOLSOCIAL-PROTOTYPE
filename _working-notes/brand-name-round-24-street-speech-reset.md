# Brand Name Round 24 - Street Speech Reset

Date: 2026-06-25

## Why Round 23 Was Rejected

Round 23 used the correct business source words:

- Demand aggregation
- Demand operating system
- Demand network
- B2B2C platform
- Socio-commerce
- Value-chain demand network
- Demand identity
- Verified proof
- Role layer
- Mission engine

But the names failed the most important test:

**Indian users will not feel easy speaking these names on the street.**

Examples rejected in spirit:

- Dovya
- Denva
- Dovara
- Ideva
- Veyna
- Veyda
- Ardan
- Ardva
- Bivan
- Sovica

Reason:

- Too technical.
- Too artificial.
- Too close to internal abbreviation logic.
- Not natural in street speech.
- Does not sound like something a chaiwala, auto captain, kirana owner, restaurant worker, mother, student, creator, or manufacturer will casually say.

## New Priority Order

The earlier order was:

1. Meaning
2. Abbreviation
3. Indian mouthfeel
4. Brand polish

This was wrong for final brand discovery.

The new order is:

1. Street speech ease
2. Indian emotional recall
3. Platform relevance
4. Brand polish
5. Hidden meaning / abbreviation
6. Trademark screening

Meaning must support the name, not make the name heavy.

## Street Speech Test

A name must feel natural in sentences like:

- "Bhai, ___ pe dekh le."
- "___ kar de."
- "___ se payment bhej."
- "___ pe kaam mil gaya."
- "___ pe order kar diya."
- "___ wale se baat kar."
- "Shop ka ___ khol."
- "Mummy bhi ___ use kar legi."
- "Captain ko ___ se ride mili."
- "Restaurant ka order ___ pe aaya."

If the sentence feels awkward, the name is rejected.

## What To Avoid Now

Avoid:

- Internal acronym names.
- Names that sound like SaaS products.
- Names that sound like pharma brands.
- Names that sound like law/finance tools.
- Names with hard consonant clusters.
- Names that require explanation before speaking.
- Names that are easy to write but not easy to say.
- Names that feel like startup naming exercise instead of Indian speech.

## What To Seek Now

Seek:

- Spoken-first names.
- Two clean beats.
- Indian but not common.
- Can become a verb.
- Can be shouted, whispered, recommended, joked with, and trusted.
- Sounds natural in Hindi-English, Gujarati, Marathi, Punjabi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Odia, and street English accents.
- Still broad enough for social, commerce, money, chat, work, mobility, business and creator layers.

## Useful Sound Shapes

Better sound shapes:

- Aana / jaana / karna / lena / dena energy, but not exact words.
- Soft syllables: aa, ya, na, ra, va, la, ma.
- Two or three beats maximum.
- No English-only clusters.
- No fake tech endings.

Possible shapes to explore:

- Naya-style but not Naya.
- Apna-style but not Apna.
- Chalo-style but not Chalo.
- Milna-style but not Milna.
- Lena/Dena-style but not Lena/Dena.
- Saath-style but not Saathi/Saath.
- Ghar-style but not Ghar.
- Kaam-style but not Kaam.

## New Naming Method

The next round should not begin from abbreviations.

It should begin from spoken Indian intent:

1. "I need something."
2. "Find it."
3. "Bring it."
4. "Book it."
5. "Pay it."
6. "Talk to them."
7. "I got work."
8. "It is trusted."
9. "It is done."
10. "Use this app."

Then create brand names that sound like natural Indian speech, but are still invented enough to own.

## Next Round Requirement

Round 25 should produce 30 names using 10 spoken-intent patterns:

1. Ask / need
2. Find / get
3. Bring / deliver
4. Pay / money
5. Book / service
6. Work / earning
7. Talk / chat
8. Trust / proof
9. Together / social
10. Done / outcome

Each pattern should produce 3 names.

Every candidate must include:

- Street sentence test.
- Pronunciation.
- Platform link.
- Risk note.
- Whether it can become a verb.

## New Rule

If a normal Indian user cannot say the name without feeling they are speaking a company presentation word, reject it immediately.
