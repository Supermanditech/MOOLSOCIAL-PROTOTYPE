# Brand Name Round 13 - Chaiwala Family Letters

User direction:

The previous names are not easy enough for a chaiwala to pronounce.

New rule:

Every name must pass the chai-stall test:

- someone sees it once
- says it immediately
- can tell another person on phone
- no spelling explanation
- no foreign polish
- no Sanskrit-heavy / religious-heavy feeling
- no baby-name feeling as the main identity

Hidden family rule:

Every name should keep at least `A` and `V` from Aarya and Vivaan.

## Names To Be Careful With

Quick public web surface checks show crowding or caution around:

- Vaya
- Viva
- Avo
- Vanu
- Viya
- Yara
- Riva
- Vana
- Aava

These are not final legal conclusions, but they should not be emotionally locked without serious trademark review.

## 10 Chaiwala-Test Names

1. **Vamo**
   - Pronunciation: va-mo.
   - Family letters: V + A.
   - Why it fits: very easy, energetic, secular, platform-friendly.
   - Test: Vamo pe daal, Vamo Pay, Vamo Work, Vamo Business.

2. **Savi**
   - Pronunciation: sa-vi.
   - Family letters: A + Vi.
   - Why it fits: soft but not too soft, very easy, friendly for consumer and creator.
   - Test: Savi pe dekh, Savi Pay, Savi Creator.

3. **Navo**
   - Pronunciation: na-vo.
   - Family letters: N + A + V.
   - Why it fits: simple, broad, all-religion friendly, not category-locked.
   - Test: Navo pe daal, Navo Work, Navo Business.

4. **Zava**
   - Pronunciation: za-va.
   - Family letters: A + V.
   - Why it fits: sharper and more national-brand style, still easy.
   - Test: Zava kar de, Zava Pay, Zava Captain.

5. **Kava**
   - Pronunciation: ka-va.
   - Family letters: A + V.
   - Why it fits: strong, clean, secular, app-icon friendly.
   - Test: Kava pe daal, Kava Work, Kava Business.

6. **Vano**
   - Pronunciation: va-no.
   - Family letters: V + A + N.
   - Why it fits: direct, simple, useful across consumer/business/work.
   - Test: Vano Pay, Vano Captain, Vano Social.

7. **Tavi**
   - Pronunciation: ta-vi.
   - Family letters: A + Vi.
   - Why it fits: short, active, easy in Indian mouth.
   - Test: Tavi kar de, Tavi Work, Tavi Creator.

8. **Sava**
   - Pronunciation: sa-va.
   - Family letters: A + V.
   - Why it fits: simple and calm, not religious-coded.
   - Test: Sava pe daal, Sava Pay, Sava Business.

9. **Vina**
   - Pronunciation: vi-na.
   - Family letters: Vi + N + A.
   - Why it fits: extremely easy, but softer/name-like.
   - Test: Vina Social, Vina Pay, Vina Work.

10. **Pavo**
    - Pronunciation: pa-vo.
    - Family letters: A + V.
    - Why it fits: simple, distinctive, and easy.
    - Test: Pavo pe daal, Pavo Business, Pavo Pay.

## Best 3

1. **Vamo**
2. **Navo**
3. **Zava**

## Current Recommendation

**Vamo**

Reason:

It is the cleanest chaiwala-mouth candidate in this round. It keeps `V` and `A`, does not sound religious, does not feel grocery-only, and has enough energy for a super-app.
