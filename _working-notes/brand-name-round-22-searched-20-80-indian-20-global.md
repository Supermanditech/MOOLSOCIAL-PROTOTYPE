# Brand Name Round 22 - Searched 20 Names Under 80% Indian / 20% Global Brief

Date: 2026-06-25

## Brief Used

The user rejected previous names as common, unprofessional, or disconnected from India and platform objective.

This round follows the updated filter:

- 80% Indian in emotion, mouthfeel, and memory.
- 20% international in polish, spelling, and brand scalability.
- Not direct dictionary Hindi/Sanskrit.
- Not a local scheme, NGO, government, religious, caste, grocery-only, taxi-only, or fintech-only name.
- Should be usable by consumer, auto captain, grocer, manufacturer, restaurant, creator, worker, and family.
- Must feel like a future large app brand, not just a meaningful word.

## Search Method

Quick public searches were run with combinations like:

- "[name] India app trademark company"
- "[name] trademark India"
- "[name] app"

This is not legal clearance. Official trademark search still requires IP India exact and phonetic search.

## Early Finding

The best pattern emerging from actual search is not random invented names. It is:

**Indian outcome verbs softened into brandable app names.**

Examples:

- Laayo
- Aavyo
- Milyo
- Lavyo

These are closer to Indian mouth memory than the earlier professional-but-empty names. They also connect to the platform objective:

- User asks.
- App finds.
- App confirms.
- App brings.
- Work/payment/social proof happens.
- Outcome arrives.

## Problem With This Pattern

The risk is that these may feel too regional, especially Gujarati/Rajasthani/Marwari leaning.

Need to test:

- Hindi belt pronunciation.
- South Indian pronunciation.
- Bengali/Odia pronunciation.
- Muslim/Christian/Sikh/Jain/Hindu neutrality.
- Investor-room professionalism.
- App icon seriousness.

They are not final. They are pattern indicators.

## My Honest View

Laayo is the most promising of this batch because it has:

- Indian mouthfeel.
- Outcome/fulfilment relation.
- A simple two-beat sound.
- Verb potential: "Laayo pe kar do."
- Platform relevance beyond grocery if positioned correctly.

But it still needs serious testing because it may sound too regional or too delivery-like.
